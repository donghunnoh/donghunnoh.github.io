ROBOCUP: Autonomous Soccer Competition with Adult-Size Humanoids

Project Overview
RoboCup, an international robotics competition, promotes robotics innovation with the ultimate goal of developing robots capable of defeating human soccer champions by 2050. RoMeLa competed with ARTEMIS, a humanoid robot equipped with muscle-like proprioceptive actuators for exceptional agility and precision. RoMeLa's pursuit of a sixth title with ARTEMIS also underscores the team's contribution to the competition's ambitious goal.
My Contributions
I participated in RoboCup 2022, held in Bangkok, and assisted with the preparations for RoboCup 2023. For both competitions, I developed a sophisticated high-level controller for soccer play using a behavior tree and a GUI for monitoring the robots' status. I successfully integrated it with other modules such as localization, a mid-level planner, and the central game controller, using shared memory and ROS/ROS2 middleware.
To achieve the goal of a fully autonomous humanoid robot, these three capabilities are essential: action, sensing, and planning. RoMeLa has advanced this cause with ARTEMIS, which demonstrates remarkable locomotion thanks to its proprioceptive actuators and the sophisticated control algorithms. Adhering to RoboCup's equitable sensor policy, which aligns with the 2050 goal of human-robot competition, ARTEMIS uses human-level sensors like stereo cameras. This ensures that the robot operates with sensory inputs analogous to human capabilities.
‍
ARTEMIS is equipped with a state estimator that leverages data from proprioceptive sensors and combines it with inputs from an IMU and stereo cameras to ascertain the position and orientation of the robot, the ball, and other elements within its environment. To translate this information into action, a rule-based high-level planner has been developed. The intricate technical details that underpin this system will be elaborated upon in the future.
